Project 01
Pathfinder — Custom Workflow CRM
TIAA · Product Owner & Program Lead · 2016 - Present · No budget. No project charter. Just a problem worth solving.

The Problem
Clients were missing mortgage payments because nobody owned the handoff.

When front office teams found solutions to client issues, there was no structured way to route them to the right back or middle office team. Resolution meant knowing the right person to email. That email often went to the wrong team. Cases sat for 3-5 days. Clients called back. Transactions failed. I heard it firsthand as a financial consultant — and when I got promoted, I decided to fix it.

What I Built
A TurboTax-style routing system. No budget. One developer. Six months.

Pathfinder is a SharePoint-backed ASPX workflow system that routes escalation cases to the correct resolution team based on a structured intake process. The front-end works like TurboTax — answer questions about the problem, get routed to the right team with full context. Built with one developer, on the side, while doing our regular jobs. No formal funding. Just a clear gap and the initiative to fill it.

The Result
120,000+ cases. $15M+ saved. Still running 200+ times a day.

Resolution time dropped from 3-5 business days to 24 hours. At $65 per complaint avoided, 120,000+ resolved cases adds up to $15M+ in savings. Today Pathfinder connects 1,000+ front-end users with 25+ back and middle office teams. It handles payments, trades, letters, and more. Gold Stevie Award winner. NICSA NOVA Award winner. Still running six years later.

The Problem
A 1,000+ user identity migration with zero tolerance for failure.

Migrating access and entitlements for over 1,000 users to a new service provider in a regulated financial services environment. Any disruption meant compliance exposure, security risk, and operational failure. The stakes were high and the timeline was fixed.

My Approach
RBAC design, cross-functional coordination, and zero-defect execution.

Led RBAC design and access provisioning architecture across IT, Cyber, Compliance, and Operations. The Result
Fully operational Day 1. Zero major defects. $25M+ in savings.

Every user was migrated on schedule with no major incidents. The program contributed to $25M+ in organizational savings through optimized vendor relationships and streamlined operations. Became the model for future large-scale access migrations at the firm.

Enterprise AI Governance Framework
TIAA · Program Architect · 2024 - Present

The Problem
AI investments were being evaluated ad hoc with no consistent framework.

As AI adoption accelerated across the firm, senior leadership had no structured way to evaluate, prioritize, or govern AI initiatives. Requests came in from every direction with no intake process, no risk assessment, and no value modeling. The result was inconsistent decisions and missed opportunities.

My Approach
Designed the intake-to-value pipeline from the ground up.

Built the full governance framework: intake process, prioritization criteria, risk assessment methodology, and value modeling templates. Created the AI Governance Dashboard that centralized all AI initiatives and gave leadership a single view of the portfolio. Partnered with Cyber, Data Engineering, and Operations to establish guardrails for responsible AI adoption.

The Result
The framework became the decision standard for AI investment across the organization.

Senior leadership adopted the framework as the primary tool for evaluating AI investments. The intake pipeline reduced ad hoc requests, improved portfolio visibility, and enabled faster, more consistent decisions. The model has since informed how the firm approaches responsible AI adoption at scale.
